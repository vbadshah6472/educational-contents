Information & Communication Technology

ICT refers to the use of combination of technologies like computers, the internet, mobile phones, and other digital devices to create/share information and communicate with others. ICT is involved in various fields such as education, healthcare, finance, and entertainment. In education, ICT helps in improving teaching and learning practices by providing tools for research, communication, and content creation.

ATM Machines

Click the image to zoom

What is an ATM?

An Automated Teller Machine (ATM) is an electronic banking outlet that allows customers to complete basic transactions without a bank teller.

Did You Know?

The first ATM was installed in London in 1967 by Barclays Bank. Today there are over 3 million ATMs worldwide!

ATMs are available 24/7, making banking convenient anytime!

Parts of an ATM

2/4

An ATM has several input and output parts that help you complete transactions. Let's identify them:

Card Reader - Reads your bank card
Keypad - For entering your PIN
Screen - Displays information
Cash Dispenser - Gives out money

Input Devices

  • Card reader
  • Keypad
  • Touch screen

Output Devices

  • Screen
  • Cash dispenser
  • Receipt printer

How ATMs Work

3/4

Let's follow the steps of how an ATM transaction works:

1

Insert Card

You insert your bank card into the card reader.

2

Enter PIN

You enter your secret PIN using the keypad.

3

Select Transaction

You choose what you want to do (withdraw, check balance, etc.)

4

Complete Transaction

The ATM communicates with your bank and completes your request.

5

Take Card & Cash

You take your card back and receive cash if requested.

What Do They Do?

  • Printers produce hard copies of digital files
  • Scanners create digital files from physical documents
  • Photocopiers duplicate physical documents quickly

Common Uses

  • Printing school assignments
  • Scanning family photos
  • Copying important documents

Many modern devices combine all these functions into one machine!

Types of Printers

2/4

There are several types of printers, each with different technologies:

Inkjet Printers

Spray tiny droplets of ink onto paper. Good for color printing and photos.

Laser Printers

Use toner powder and heat. Faster and better for text documents.

Dot Matrix

Older technology that uses pins to strike ink ribbon. Still used for carbon copies.

3D Printers

Create three-dimensional objects by adding material layer by layer.

Quick Comparison

Type Best For Speed Cost
Inkjet Photos, color Medium Low initial, high ink
Laser Text, office Fast Higher initial, lower per page
3D Objects, prototypes Very slow Expensive

Using Printers & Scanners

3/4

Follow these steps to use printers and scanners:

Printing a Document

  1. Open the document you want to print
  2. Click "File" then "Print" or press Ctrl+P
  3. Connect your computer with Printer using USB cable.
  4. Then, Select your printer from the list
  5. Choose printing options (pages, copies, etc.)
  6. Click "Print" and wait for your document

Scanning a Document

  1. Place your document on the scanner glass
  2. Open your scanning software
  3. Select scan settings (color, resolution, etc.)
  4. Click "Scan" and save your digital file

What is a Router?

A router is a device that connects multiple networks and directs internet traffic between them.

What is Wi-Fi?

Wi-Fi is a wireless technology that uses radio waves to provide network connectivity.

Most homes use a combination modem/router that connects to the internet and broadcasts Wi-Fi.

How Routers & Wi-Fi Work

Key Components

Antennas

Broadcast Wi-Fi signal in all directions

Power Port

Connects router to electricity

WAN Port

What is a Set-Top Box?

A device that connects to a TV and external signal source, converting the signal into content for display.

Types of Set-Top Boxes

  • Cable TV boxes: For traditional cable service
  • Satellite receivers: For satellite TV service
  • Digital converters: For over-the-air TV signals
  • Streaming devices: Like Apple TV, Fire Stick

What is a Website?

A website is a collection of web pages and related content identified by a common domain name and published on at least one web server.

Popular Examples

Google.com Wikipedia.org YouTube.com Facebook.com

The first website was published in 1991 by Tim Berners-Lee, the inventor of the World Wide Web.

Search Engines

2/4

How Search Engines Work

1

Crawling

Bots (called spiders) visit websites and follow links to discover pages.

2

Indexing

Information from pages is stored in a massive database (the index).

3

Ranking

When you search, the engine finds matching pages and ranks them by relevance.

Popular Search Engines

Google

Bing

Yahoo

DuckDuckGo

Search Tips

  • Use quotes for exact phrases: "climate change"
  • Use minus to exclude words: jaguar -car
  • Use site: to search one site: site:wikipedia.org nepal
  • Use filetype: to find specific files: filetype:pdf

Internet Service Providers

3/4

What is an ISP?

An Internet Service Provider (ISP) is a company that provides access to the internet.

Types of Internet Connections

  • DSL: Uses phone lines
  • Cable: Uses TV cable lines
  • Fiber: Uses optical fiber (fastest)
  • Satellite: For remote areas
  • Mobile: 3G/4G/5G networks

Choosing an ISP

Considerations
  • Speed requirements
  • Data limits
  • Price
  • Reliability
Typical Speeds
  • Basic: 5-10 Mbps
  • Standard: 20-50 Mbps
  • Premium: 100+ Mbps
  • Fiber: 300 Mbps - 1 Gbps

What is Social Media?

Websites and applications that enable users to create and share content or to participate in social networking.

Key Features

  • User profiles
  • Friend/connection systems
  • Content sharing
  • Messaging
  • News feeds

Over 4.8 billion people worldwide use social media - that's about 60% of the global population!

Advantages of Social Media

2/4

Connection

  • Stay in touch with friends and family
  • Connect with people worldwide
  • Find people with similar interests

Education

  • Access to educational content
  • Join study groups
  • Follow experts in various fields

Awareness

  • Learn about current events
  • Raise awareness for causes
  • Get news quickly

Opportunities

  • Showcase talents
  • Find job opportunities
  • Promote businesses

Positive Uses

Fundraising Skill Sharing Support Groups Creative Expression Cultural Exchange

Disadvantages of Social Media

3/4

Privacy Risks

  • Personal information exposure
  • Data collection by companies
  • Location tracking

Cyberbullying

  • Harassment online
  • Spread of rumors
  • Exclusion from groups

Time Wasting

  • Endless scrolling
  • Reduced productivity
  • Less face-to-face interaction

Misinformation

  • Spread of fake news
  • Echo chambers
  • Manipulation

Negative Effects

Addiction Anxiety Sleep Problems Body Image Issues FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)

Safety Tips

  • Use strong, unique passwords
  • Adjust privacy settings
  • Be careful what you share
  • Think before you post
  • Limit personal information

Blogs

1/4

People sharing ideas through blogs

What is a Blog?

A blog is a regularly updated website or web page, typically run by an individual or small group, that is written in an informal or conversational style.

Common Blog Types

  • Personal blogs
  • Travel blogs
  • Food blogs
  • Educational blogs
  • News blogs

The word "blog" comes from "weblog" - a log of information on the web!

Creating a Blog

2/4

Steps to Start a Blog

  1. Choose a topic: What will you write about?
  2. Pick a platform: Blogger, WordPress, Wix, etc.
  3. Select a domain name: Your blog's web address
  4. Design your blog: Choose a template and layout
  5. Write your first post: Share your thoughts!
  6. Publish and share: Let people know about it
